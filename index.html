Interesting fact about camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • The amount of water a camel drinks on a day-to-day basis can vary greatly, as they drink to replace only the fluid they’ve lost. A thirsty camel can drink up to 135 liters in one sitting.

Interesting fact about chameleon>

  • THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.
  • SKIN CRYSTALS ENABLE THEM TO CHANGE COLOR AT WILL.
  • UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN’T REGROW THEIR TAILS.

Interesting fact about pigeon>

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

Interesting fact about teddy-bear>

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.

Interesting fact about panda>

  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • Bamboo is critical to their diet
  • But they do occasionally eat something other than bamboo.

Interesting fact about flying-cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  • They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
